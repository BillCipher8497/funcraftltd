About Us

How a question during lockdown became a place where talent is recognised, nurtured and celebrated.

Our story

From a lockdown question to a lasting mission

Kalavaibhav Sevabhavi Sanstha was founded in March 2022, and its beginning has a small but heartfelt story. During the COVID-19 lockdown, everyone faced the same question: how do we keep special students engaged?

With that in mind, we began running online sessions for them — music, dance, yoga, and more. Those sessions revealed something important: these students needed a dedicated hobby and arts class, a space where their physical and mental development could grow through creativity.

That idea became an organisation, founded originally under the name Funcraft.

The hobby class — Chhandakala Varg

Talent, given the right approach

Like all children, differently-abled individuals are full of artistic talent — and with the right approach, that talent can be nurtured into genuine growth. They can express themselves. They can experience pure joy.

To make that possible, a hobby and arts class for differently-abled individuals was started in the Sahakarnagar area of Pune. Through dance, drama, singing, painting, handicrafts, physical activities, yoga, and music therapy, students develop both physically and mentally.

The class is led by experienced teachers and counsellors — many with years of work in the field of disability — who volunteer their time selflessly.

Beyond the classroom

Special initiatives

A selection of the experiences, celebrations and outings we have organised for our students, parents and members.

  • Honouring the mothers of our special students during Navratri.
  • A metro-ride experience for students (Pune to Pimpri-Chinchwad).
  • A Navratri felicitation of accomplished women working in the field of disability.
  • Nature visit to Taljai Garden (students, parents and members).
  • A kirtan by Mrs. Manasi Joshi on Guru Purnima.
  • Mall visit giving students hands-on, real-world shopping experience.
  • Group outings to Prathamesh Resort and Madhurangan Resort (residential camp).
  • A "Blades of Glory" cricket museum visit.
  • A talk by Mrs. Seema Dabke on the lives of soldiers, for Independence Day.
  • Aukshan (traditional honouring) of parents on International Women's Day.
  • Counselling-focused music therapy for parents, plus a talk on legal guardianship.
  • Painting Ganpati idols on MDF board (students and parents together).
  • An annual get-together (Snehsammelan).
